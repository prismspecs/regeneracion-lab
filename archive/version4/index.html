REGENERACIÓN LAB A PRACTICE OF PLACE

by the collective

An anti-colonial research lab bringing together scholars, artists, and activists to explore Indigenous sovereignty, decolonial practice, border politics, and cultural resistance.

Named after the anarchist newspaper published by the Flores Magón brothers from 1900-1918, Regeneración Lab carries forward their spirit of radical critique while centering Indigenous epistemologies often erased in traditional anarchist narratives.

We create space for research, dialogue, and cultural production that challenges settler-colonial frameworks and imagines otherwise. Our work spans multiple domains: from Indigenous mapping practices and counter-cartography to water justice and environmental resistance.

A Practice of Place

Place-based knowledge and Indigenous practices offer pathways to understanding our relationships with land, water, and each other beyond colonial boundaries. This work requires sustained attention to the particular—to the specificities of history, language, and lived experience that settler colonial systems attempt to erase or subsume.

Through collaborative research, artistic practice, and community engagement, we explore how anti-colonial praxis might take shape in the present moment. What does it mean to season ourselves to a place? To learn its rhythms, honor its relations, and practice forms of care that resist extraction?

"We create space for research, dialogue, and cultural production that challenges settler-colonial frameworks and imagines otherwise."

Current Work

Our current projects include an Indigenous Border Studies syllabus, a community historical archive preserving tribal histories and oral testimonies, and research into water justice and technological sovereignty. We support resident scholars and artists working at these intersections.

The lab operates through a commitment to reciprocity, accountability, and the belief that decolonization is not a metaphor but an ongoing practice requiring material transformation of relationships to land, resources, and power.
