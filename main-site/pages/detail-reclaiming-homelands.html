Reclaiming Homelands: Indigenous North San Diego County

2019 • UC Critical Mission Studies Initiative

Collaborative project with Payómkawichum and Kumeyaay youth to recover the Indigenous place-names of the North San Diego County "Mission Trail", part of the UC Critical Mission Studies Initiative.

About the Project

This project centers the voices and knowledge of Payómkawichum (Luiseño) and Kumeyaay youth in recovering and reclaiming Indigenous place-names that have been erased or replaced by Spanish colonial mission names. Through community-based research and mapping, youth researchers worked to document the original Indigenous geographies of North San Diego County.

The project challenges the dominant narrative of California's "Mission Trail" by foregrounding Indigenous histories, land relations, and ongoing presence. By recovering place-names, the project asserts Indigenous sovereignty and connection to ancestral homelands.
